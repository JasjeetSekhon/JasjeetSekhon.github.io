The Varying Role of Voter Information Across Democratic Societies

Jasjeet S. Sekhon

This website is for the distribution of material related to my book project on the varying role of voter information across democratic societies. Using new robust matching methods for making causal inferences from survey data, I demonstrate that there are profound differences between how voters behave in mature democracies versus how they behave in new ones. The problems of voter ignorance and inattentiveness are not as serious in mature democracies as many analysts have suggested but are of grave concern in new democracies. Citizens in mature democracies are able to accomplish something that citizens in fledgling democracies are not: inattentive and poorly informed citizens are able to vote like their better informed compatriots and hence need to pay little attention to political events such as election campaigns in order to vote as if they were attentive.

The following working paper summarizes the argument and some of the results:
The Varying Role of Voter Information across Democratic Societies.
